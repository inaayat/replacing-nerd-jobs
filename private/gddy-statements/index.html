GoDaddy Inc. (NYSE: GDDY) — CIK 0001609711. Internet domain registrar and web hosting company. Revenue segments: Applications & Commerce, Core Platform. All filings below are from SEC EDGAR and are publicly accessible.

Practice Exercises

1. Pull the last 5 years of income statements and build a revenue forecast (top-line growth model) In Progress
Use the Full Data download below. Pull Total Revenue for FY2020–FY2025, calculate YoY growth rates, and build a simple top-line forecast for FY2026–FY2028. Consider: is growth accelerating or decelerating? What ARPU and customer count assumptions drive your estimate?
Learnings: —
2. Build a 3-statement model linking income statement → balance sheet → cash flow Not Started
Start with the income statement, flow net income into retained earnings on the balance sheet, then build the cash flow statement from changes in BS line items. The model should balance: Assets = Liabilities + Equity, and ending cash should tie to the BS.
Learnings: —
3. Take Q1–Q3 actuals and forecast Q4 + full year — then compare to the actual 10-K Not Started
Use FY2024 quarterly reports (10-Q) for Q1–Q3. Forecast Q4 based on seasonality patterns and guidance. Then compare your full-year estimate to the actual FY2024 10-K. How far off were you? Why?
Learnings: —
4. Do a variance analysis: compare your forecast to actuals and explain the gaps Not Started
After exercise 3, quantify each variance (revenue, COGS, OpEx line items). Categorize as: volume, price/mix, timing, or one-time. Build a bridge from your forecast to actuals and write a 1-page executive summary explaining the deltas.
Learnings: —
5. Build a waterfall chart showing revenue bridges (price vs. volume vs. mix) Not Started
Decompose YoY revenue growth into: customer count change (volume), ARPU change (price), and segment mix shift (Applications & Commerce vs. Core Platform). Visualize as a waterfall chart in Excel. Which driver matters most?
Learnings: —
6. Calculate key metrics: ARPU, customer count growth, retention/churn, free cash flow conversion Not Started
From the filings, calculate: ARPU (revenue / customers), implied churn (if customer count is flat but bookings grow, what does that mean?), FCF conversion (FCF / Net Income), and Rule of 40 (revenue growth % + FCF margin %). How does GDDY score vs. SaaS benchmarks?
Learnings: —
