Cancellations are accelerating

median days to cancellation · 1980–2024
Median days between a show's first and last air date, for cancelled shows only. The shaded band shows the interquartile range (middle 50% of shows). Post-2013 — when Netflix launched original programming — cancellation timelines collapsed dramatically. By the 2020s the median had fallen to 42 days, down from over 300 days in the early 2000s. Red dots mark years in the streaming era.

Does a country's population size affect cancellation speed?

21 countries · log population vs median cancel days
Each point is a country. X-axis is population (log scale); Y-axis is the median number of days a cancelled show ran before being cut. Bubble size reflects total number of cancellations. There is no strong positive correlation between population and show longevity — larger markets don't protect shows more. Germany (DE) is a striking outlier: a large country with relatively long cancellation windows. Japan (JP) and Brazil (BR) are cut fast despite large populations.
